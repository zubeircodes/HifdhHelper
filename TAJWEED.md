## Tajweed Rules Summary

### What is Tajweed?

Tajweed (ÿ™ÿ¨ŸàŸäÿØ) means "to make better" or "to improve." It's the set of rules governing the pronunciation of the Quran during recitation.

### Why Learn Tajweed?

1. **Preserve Meaning**: Correct pronunciation preserves the intended meaning
2. **Beauty**: Makes recitation melodious and beautiful
3. **Prophetic Tradition**: Following how the Prophet Muhammad (Ô∑∫) recited
4. **Spiritual Connection**: Deeper connection with the words of Allah

### Main Categories

1. **Noon Sakinah & Tanween Rules**
   - Idgham (merging)
   - Iqlab (conversion)
   - Ikhfa (hiding)
   - Izhar (clear pronunciation)

2. **Meem Sakinah Rules**
   - Idgham Shafawi
   - Ikhfa Shafawi
   - Izhar Shafawi

3. **Madda (Elongation)**
   - Natural (2 counts)
   - Permissible (2, 4, or 6 counts)
   - Necessary (6 counts)
   - Connected/Separated

4. **Qalqalah (Echoing)**
   - Light (middle of word)
   - Heavy (end of word with sukoon)

5. **Lam Rules**
   - Lam Shamsiyyah (sun letters - assimilated)
   - Lam Qamariyyah (moon letters - pronounced)

## How to Use This App for Learning

1. **Start with familiar surahs** (like Al-Fatihah or short surahs)
2. **Observe the colors** while listening to the recitation
3. **Notice patterns** in how rules are applied
4. **Practice repetition** - use the repeat feature to master each verse
5. **Listen carefully** to Sheikh Mishary Alafasy's pronunciation
6. **Match colors to sounds** - connect visual rules with audio

## Tips for Beginners

- Don't be overwhelmed by all the colors at first
- Focus on one rule at a time
- Start with the madda (elongation) rules - they're most noticeable
- Listen more than you read initially
- Use the repetition feature to train your ear
- Practice with a qualified teacher when possible

## Resources for Deep Learning

While this app provides visual Tajweed markers, we recommend:
- Taking formal Tajweed classes
- Practicing with a qualified teacher
- Using this app as a supplement to structured learning
- Regular recitation practice
